## Bot Stance Configurations

The `^stance` command requires a numerical ID argument. The mappings and class-based defaults are detailed below.

### Stance ID Mappings
- **`0` (Passive):** The bot will not cast any spells or engage in combat.
- **`1` (Balanced):** The default AI behavior. Bot fights and casts spells normally.
- **`2` (Efficient):** Focuses on conserving mana while performing essential duties (heals, slows, buffs).
- **`3` (Reactive):** A middle ground with more varied spell selection and reactive targeting.
- **`4` (Aggressive):** Offensive stance, makes less effort to conserve mana.
- **`5` (Burn):** All-out offensive stance. Spams high-damage abilities and spells.
- **`6` (BurnAE):** All-out offensive stance, including the use of Area of Effect (AE) spells.

### Default Stances for All Classes
When a bot is first created or spawned without a saved stance in the database, the server automatically assigns a class-specific default stance. The default stances for each class are detailed in the table below:

## Stay at Camp and Pull Mobs Setup
To command your bots to remain at a designated camp spot while a player (or puller bot) pulls enemies to them:

1. **Set Camp Location (`^guard`)**:
   Move your bots to the desired camp spot and type:
   `^guard` (or target individual bots and do `^guard` for each, or use the group-wide guard command).
   This anchors the bots to their current coordinates. They will stand still and won't follow you when you run off to pull.

2. **Prevent Premature Engagement (`^hold`)**:
   By default, even when guarding, if a bot detects an enemy or if you get hit, they might run out to attack it. To prevent them from running out of camp prematurely:
   `^hold`
   This tells the bots not to attack anything until you release them.

3. **Pulling the Mobs**:
   Run out, pull the target mob(s), and run back into the camp boundary.

4. **Engaging the Mobs (`^attack` or `^release`)**:
   Once the mob is inside the camp area, order the bots to attack:
   `^atk` (or `^attack` with a specific target)
   Or, if they were on hold, release them:
   `^release`
   This resumes their standard AI combat logic, and since they are guarding the camp, they will fight at the camp spot.

5. **Resuming Follow (`^follow`)**:
   Once combat is over and you want to move the camp, type:
   `^follow`
   This breaks their guard anchor and orders them to follow you again.
