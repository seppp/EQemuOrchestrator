# EverQuest 72-Man Raid Theorycraft (Up to Luclin)

When theorycrafting an ideal 72-character raid that can steamroll everything from Classic up through the Shadows of Luclin expansion (including Vex Thal and Emperor Ssraeshza), we have to consider a few major mechanics from that era:
1. **Resist Checks:** Velious dragons and Luclin bosses have devastating AoEs. High resists are mandatory.
2. **The "Bowquest" Meta:** Luclin introduces the Archery Mastery III (AM3) and Endless Quiver Alternate Advancements (AAs). Rangers become arguably the highest sustained, safest DPS in the game.
3. **Botting Synergy:** Since you are orchestrating this with `RGMercs`, we want to favor ranged DPS and passive buffs (like Bards) over positional melee (Rogues/Monks) that require complex pathing and facing to maximize backstabs.

Here is the ideal, RGMercs-optimized 72-character raid composition.

---

## The Golden Ratio (Class Breakdown)

*   **Bards (12):** One in *every single group*. RGMercs bards twist perfectly. They provide unparalleled resist buffs, mana regeneration, and overhaste without needing manual intervention.
*   **Clerics (10):** The backbone. You need a solid 4-to-5-man Complete Heal (CH) chain for the Main Tank on bosses like Emperor Ssraeshza, plus backup chains for Off-Tanks and group healing.
*   **Rangers (15):** The ultimate bot DPS in Luclin. With AAs, they just `/autofire` from maximum range. They never get hit by close-range AoEs, don't suffer ripostes, and don't care which way the boss is facing.
*   **Wizards (8):** Essential for unresisted lures, burst DPS, and specifically for **Bane Damage** on Emperor Ssraeshza.
*   **Magicians (5):** Their most important job for a bot army is **Call of the Hero (CoH)**. Moving 72 bots through the maze of Vex Thal or Ssra Temple is a nightmare; having Mages CoH the raid saves hours. Also great for Mod Rods and fire-and-forget pet DPS.
*   **Warriors (3):** The undisputed kings of raid tanking due to their `Defensive` discipline.
*   **Enchanters (4):** Crowd control (Mez) for trash/adds, Tashanian for magic resist debuffing, and KEI / Visions of Grandeur. 
*   **Shamans (4):** Malo (resist debuff), Slow (Turgur's Insects), and massive stat buffs (Focus / Avatar) for the tanks and melee.
*   **Necromancers (3):** Sustained DPS and "Twitch" mana batteries for the CH chain clerics during long boss fights.
*   **Druids (3):** Backup heals, damage shields, crucial Luclin resist buffs (Circle of Seasons), and evacs.
*   **Monks (2):** Pullers. RGMercs or MQ2Nav can handle pulling, but having a Monk's Feign Death and Mend makes splitting raid targets much easier.
*   **Shadowknights (2):** Unmatched snap-aggro for locking down adds instantly. 
*   **Paladins (1):** LoH (Lay on Hands) is a great emergency save for the Main Tank, plus group heals and cures.

## Why this setup excels up to Luclin

1. **RGMercs Friendly:** Melee DPS like Rogues are incredible in Velious and Luclin, but they require the bot to perfectly stay behind the mob to Backstab. If the mob turns, they lose massive DPS. Rangers, Wizards, and Mages don't care about positioning, making them exponentially more reliable in a bot raid.
2. **Resist Cap Saturation:** By Velious and Luclin, bosses will wipe your raid with unresistable AE effects if you aren't prepared. By slotting one Bard in every group, the entire raid gets overlapping resist songs, practically nullifying most AoE mechanics.
3. **Logistics Handled:** Moving 72 characters manually is painful. By spreading 5 Magicians across the raid, you can run just the Magicians to the boss room and use **Call of the Hero** to summon the remaining 67 bots directly to the camp, bypassing trash respawns and traps.
4. **Mana Endurance:** The Necromancer/Enchanter/Bard combo in the Cleric groups means your Complete Heal chain will literally never run out of mana, allowing you to sustain through the longest, highest-HP boss encounters in the game (like Lord Inquisitor Seru and Emperor Ssra).
