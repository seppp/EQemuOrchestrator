# The 18-Character Bootstrapper: PBAoE Farming & Power Leveling

If you are starting from absolute zero and want to bootstrap an entire 72-man raid, **PBAoE (Point Blank Area of Effect) farming is hands-down the fastest way to generate experience and farm drops in EverQuest.** 

Instead of killing 1 mob every 30 seconds, a PBAoE group pulls 60 mobs at once, perfectly chain-stuns them, and kills them all in 15 seconds. This 3-group (18 character) team is designed to be your "First Cohort." They will level to max incredibly fast, farm the initial gear, and then serve as the engine to power-level the remaining 54 characters of your raid.

---

## 1. The 3-Group Composition (18 Characters)

### 🌀 Group 1: The Engine (Control & Stun-Lock)
This group handles gathering the massive trains, keeping the puller alive, and executing the core stun rotation.
*   **Shadowknight:** The Puller. Uses snap aggro, Feign Death, and defensive discs.
*   **Cleric 1:** The anchor. Times a Complete Heal to land on the SK the exact second they stop running.
*   **Enchanter 1:** Primary Stunner (*Color Skew*).
*   **Enchanter 2:** Secondary Stunner (*Color Shift*).
*   **Enchanter 3:** Tertiary Stunner (*Color Flux*). (Having 3 Enchanters in the main group ensures a flawless rotation).
*   **Bard 1:** Plays AC, Resists, and Movement Speed for the puller.

### 💥 Group 2: The Executioners (Artillery)
Once the Enchanters lock the 60 mobs in a stun-loop, this group obliterates them.
*   **Wizard 1:** Primary PBAoE Nuke (*Supernova*, *Jyll's*).
*   **Wizard 2:** PBAoE Nuke.
*   **Wizard 3:** PBAoE Nuke.
*   **Wizard 4:** PBAoE Nuke.
*   **Magician:** PBAoE Nuke / **Call of the Hero** (essential for teleporting your low-level PL targets later).
*   **Bard 2:** Plays Mana song and Resists for the casters.

### 🔋 Group 3: Support & Sustain (The Safety Net)
This group ensures the raid never runs out of mana and recovers instantly if a pull goes wrong.
*   **Enchanter 4:** The Debuffer/Safety Net. Focuses purely on chain-casting *Tashanian* (magic resist debuff) on the train, keeping *KEI* on the raid, and emergency single-target Mez if a named mob resists the AEs.
*   **Cleric 2:** Backup Heals / *Divine Aura* rescue for bad pulls / Resurrections.
*   **Druid:** Damage Shields (for Power Leveling), Snare, and Evacs/Ports.
*   **Shaman:** Slows for named boss mobs, massive HP/Stat buffs for the SK.
*   **Necromancer:** "Twitching" (feeding mana directly to the Enchanters/Clerics) and PBAoE life taps.
*   **Bard 3:** Plays Mana / Resists.

## 2. The 1-60 Leveling Roadmap

Enchanters get their PBAoE stuns at levels 4, 16, and 30. Wizards don't get their first real PBAoE until level 12. You must adapt your strategy as you level.

### Levels 1 - 12: "The Swarm"
At this stage, you aren't AEing. You are a roaming ball of death.
*   **The Strategy:** Set your Shadowknight as the Main Assist. Every time your SK targets a mob, 17 other characters instantly single-target nuke or melee it. Mobs die instantly.
*   **Zones:** Any starter area (Field of Bone, Butcherblock) into **Kurn's Tower** or **Crushbone**.

### Levels 12 - 25: The Transition
Wizards get their first PBAoE; Enchanters have two stuns.
*   **The Strategy:** Move to **Paludal Caverns** (Luclin). Have the SK pull 5-10 bandits at a time. The Enchanters start their stun rotation, and Wizards drop small PBAoEs. 

### Levels 25 - 40: The Engine Comes Online
Enchanters get *Color Skew* at level 30 (giving them 3 stuns for a permanent chain-stun lock). Wizards get massive spells like *Circle of Force*.
*   **Zones:** **Highkeep (Goblin Basement)** or **City of Mist** (courtyard). You can now pull 20-30 mobs safely.

### Levels 40 - 52: The Meat Grinder
*   **Zones:** **Chardok** (Entrance fort and bridge) or **Old Sebilis** (Bugs / Disco). The SK pulls 40+ mobs, the Enchanters stun, and the Wizards delete them in 15 seconds.

### Levels 52 - 60: The Luclin Endgame
*   **Zones:** **The Deep (Shrieker Bridge)** or **Acrylia Caverns**. These zones yield absurd amounts of AA and Level XP.

## 3. The Gearing Strategy (Zero to Hero)

Gearing 18 characters from absolute scratch sounds daunting, but it is entirely bypassed by one fundamental EverQuest mechanic: **Casters do not need gear to function.**

### The Secret of Casters
A naked Level 50 Wizard's *Supernova* spell does the exact same damage as a fully-geared Level 50 Wizard's *Supernova*. A naked Enchanter's stun lasts just as long.
The only thing gear gives casters is Mana and HP. In this 18-man setup, your mana is supplemented by Bards (Mana song), Magicians (Mod Rods), and Necromancers (Twitch), completely negating the need for huge mana pools. 
**You can literally AE farm to level 60 with 15 naked casters.**

### Prioritize the Puller (The Shadowknight)
90% of your gearing effort should go toward the Shadowknight, and the remaining 10% toward the Cleric (to ensure they don't get one-shot if a mob breaks stun). The SK needs AC, HP, and Agility to survive the run back to camp with 50 mobs hitting them in the back.

### Targeted Farming Drops
Since you have 18 characters, you can walk into any group dungeon and instantly kill named mobs for gear.
*   **Levels 15-35:** Farm **Estate of Unrest** or **Upper Guk**. Your main focus here is getting basic Bronze/Banded armor and a *Bloodstained Tunic* for the SK. Before this, just use whatever drops; casters require exactly zero gear to function.
*   **Levels 35-45:** Farm **Lower Guk**. Kill the Froglok King and Ghoul Lord for easy mid-level tank/cleric gear.
*   **Levels 45-55:** While AEing in **Sebilis** or **Chardok**, simply loot the named mobs that spawn in your AE pulls.
*   **Levels 55-60 (The Armor Farm):** Take the 18 characters into **Kael Drakkel** (The Plate House or Arena). You can AE farm the giants. They drop massive amounts of faction and armor gems. Turn these gems in for *Thurgadin Quest Armor* for your SK and Clerics.

### The Platinum Factory
AE farming generates massive amounts of raw platinum (gems, fine steel weapons, vendor trash). By level 60, this 18-man group will have farmed tens of thousands of plat simply by vendoring the loot from 10,000 dead mobs. You can use this plat to buy your essential spells (which gets expensive for 18 characters) and purchase player-crafted gear (like cultural armor) for your SK.

### Key Items, Clickies, and Epics
While raw stats on casters don't matter as much, certain items provide massive utility and should be actively hunted once your group is capable.

**Bard Instruments (Priority for Bards):**
Bards are the backbone of your mana regeneration and puller survival. Modifiers from instruments dramatically increase song effectiveness.
*   **Percussion:** *Selo's Drums of the March* (Lower Guk - dropped by the frenzied ghoul, easily farmable at level 40+) or *Walrus Skin Drum* (Kael Drakkel - random walrus drop, farmable at 50+). Eventually upgrade to *Drums of the Beast* (Siren's Grotto - random siren drop, farmable at 55+). Crucial for movement speed.
*   **String:** *Lute of the Gypsy Princess* (Mistmoore - dropped by the gypsy princess, farmable at 30+), then *Lute of the Howler* (Velketor's Labyrinth - dropped by Icepaw kobolds, farmable at 55+). Crucial for HP/Mana regen songs.
*   **Wind/Brass:** Not as critical for the AE setup, but grab what you can for resists.

**Mana Regen (Flowing Thought) for Casters:**
*   You will want to stack **Flowing Thought** (FT) items to boost passive mana regen.
*   Look for items like the *Earring of the Solstice* (Luclin Tradeskill quest, can be done at level 50+), or farm Velious/Luclin named mobs for FT items (e.g., *Eye of Narandi* from the 10th Ring War in Great Divide at level 60).

**Essential Clickies:**
*   **Shrink Clickies:** *Bracelet of the Shadow Hive* (Vex Thal - farmable at 60) or *Wand of Imperceptibility* (Tinkered player-made item, usable by casters). For the SK early on, simply buy **Alchemy Shrink Potions** in the Bazaar. Getting 18 characters through narrow dungeon corridors requires everyone to be shrunk.
*   **Movement:** *Journeyman's Boots* (JBoots) for the SK puller to maintain speed without relying purely on the bard. (Quest involves Hasten Bootstrutter in Rathe Mountains and the Ancient Cyclops ring; realistically doable around level 35+).
*   **Invisibility/Levitate:** *Pegasus Feather Cloak* (Levitate - South Karana, dropped by Quillmane, farmable at 35+) and *Goblin Gazughi Ring* (Instant Invis - Lake of Ill Omen, dropped by Sarnak Courier, farmable at 25+) are great utility for your puller and support team.

**Epic Weapons (1.0):**
As your 18-man army reaches level 60, Epic 1.0 quests become trivial as you can easily kill the raid targets yourself. Prioritize these:
*   **Cleric Epic (*Water Sprinkler of Nem Ankh*):** Provides a manaless, 96% resurrection clicky. Essential for recovering quickly from AE wipes. (Requires farming Overking Bathezid in Chardok and Lord Bergurgle in Lake Rathe, doable with the full team at 55+).
*   **Bard Epic (*Singing Short Sword*):** Acts as all instrument types combined with a massive modifier, freeing up the Bard's hands to equip weapons while singing. (Requires killing Trakanon in Sebilis and the white dragon Lady Vox, doable with the team at 60).
*   **Enchanter Epic (*Staff of the Serpent*):** Provides a free, massive Haste/Stat buff for your SK or melee additions later. (Requires killing the Wraith of a Shissar in Fear, doable at 60).

## 4. Power Leveling the Rest of the Raid

Once this 18-character squad is max level (60), use them to power level the remaining 54 characters:

### Phase 1: Level 1 to 40 (The "Damage Shield" Method)
1. Group up 5 low-level PL targets.
2. Have your Level 60 **Druid** cast their absolute highest Damage Shield and Regeneration on one low level.
3. Have your Level 60 **Cleric** sit *out-of-group* and spam heals on that low level.
4. The low-level character pulls 20 mobs. The mobs hit the low-level character and die instantly to the Level 60 Damage Shield. 
5. Because out-of-group DS damage counts as the character's damage who is wearing the buff, the low-level group gets 100% of the XP for the kill. 

### Phase 2: Level 40 to 60 (The "PBAoE Carry" Method)
1. Break down your 18-man AE squad into new groups: **1 Enchanter, 1 Cleric, 1 Wizard**, and **3 PL Targets**.
2. Go to an AE hotspot (Chardok, The Deep).
3. The PL targets sit in the corner safely doing nothing.
4. The SK pulls the zone. The Enchanter stuns. The Wizard nukes. The Cleric heals.
5. The 3 PL targets soak up massive amounts of experience as 50+ mobs die every 10 minutes.
